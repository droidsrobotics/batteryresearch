The Battery Recycling Process

Learn about all the stages in the battery recycling process from manufacturing all the way through collecting the raw materials from a used battery.

  • Manufacturing

    Energizer, Duracell, Panasonic

    We talked to to leading battery manufacturers to learn about issues they face. Majority of batteries manufactured are still alkalines. Since they no longer contain mercury, they are not harmful for the environment. We learned about Energizer's EcoAdvanced Battery (4% reused battery material). We also learned about how battery manufacturers are pushing for legislation in the US. Currenty, only in California and Vermont are there laws about recycling one-time use batteries.

  • Consumption

    You and Me

    We did a survey of 250 people and contacted FLL teams around the world to ask them about their battery recycling habits. The results were suprising. People in the US are not aware of what the battery recycling laws are in their state, and find it very hard. In Europe and South America, there are a lot more collection locations for all types of batteries (alkaline and rechargeable) compared to the US.

  • Collection

    Call2Recycle, TerraCycle

    After talking to Call2Recycle and TerraCycle, and learned about paid and free used battery collection systems. We learned that battery manufacturers cover the cost of the collection for Call2Recycle. We visited collection locations at Best Buy, Home Depot and Staples and discovered that the collection boxes are often hidden and that people rarely follow the rules (place alkalines in a rechargeable-only box). Collection boxes are filled with mixed battery chemisteries even when they are supposed to be just for a particular type. While there are collection locations, they are not managed well and not in places people visit on a daily basis (like a school or grocery store).

  • Sorting

    Battery Solutions

    We visited two of the largest battery recyclers in the world. Both places start with batteries in boxes or buckets. They sort the batteries by hand into different chemistries - a slow and expensive process. Battery Solutions does have the only battery sorting machine in US (by Refind) for large volume containers, but this machine has its limitations. The raw materials from an alkaline battery are not worth much, so they both charge fees to sort and process the alkalines.

  • Processing

    Inmetco

    We saw each stage of the process in recovering the raw materials. The zinc, manganese and steel are all recovered and used in other industries.

  • Reuse

    Inmectco, Battery Solutions

    Battery Solutions and Inmetco taught us about what happens next with the raw materials. Some of it is used back in batteries. The rest is used in the steel, brick and fertilizer industries.

Big Problems in Battery Recycling

80% of all batteries manufactured are alkalines and 90% of all batteries end up in US landfills. US consumers don't recycle batteries enough!


Increase Used Battery Collection

There needs to be more battery collection locations in the United States. Adding them to schools, community centers, libraries and street corners are important. Several of the FLL teams we partnered with were working on this problem.

Reduce the number of alkalines used

Encouraging consumers to use rechargeables is also a great step in reducing the number of discarded batteries. Another one of our FLL friends worked on this problem.

Reduce the number of batteries discarded

Provide ways to make sure the batteries are completely spent (used). Battery Solutions informed us that most batteries tend to have charge left in them.

The Bigger Problem!

The raw materials in alkalines are not worth the cost to recycle them. So the big question is...

Battery Sorting

Sorting is done by hand!

Step 1 Workers have to be hired and trained to identify different batteries by chemistry.

Step 2 Workers cut open boxes or open buckets of batteries. They sort them into all the different chemistries (6+ chemistries) by hand.

Step 3 Workers track each type of battery so that they can provide this information to the person who sent them the used batteries.

With ReFind (only at Battery Solutions):

Step 1 Workers start with larger drums of batteries. They have to hand sort the batteries to separate cylindrical and 9V batteries from the rest.

Step 2 The sorted batteries are loaded into the Refind machine to sort again.

Step 3 The machine will sort the battery if it has an image of it. Otherwise, it will reject the battery. The machine will also record the brand, size, type, etc.
