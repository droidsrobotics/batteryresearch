battery life cycle What happens to the battery once you are done with it?

Once a battery is spent, 98% of them end up in landfill. 2% end up being recycled. These batteries are collected in limited collection locations in the United States. They are then shipped to processing facilties. At the processing facility, they are sorted into different chemistries. Once sorted, these batteries are recycled for the raw materials they contain. For an alkaline battery, this includes the steel, manganese, zinc, paper and plastic. These materials are used in other industries. Energizer's EcoAdvanced uses 4% recycled battery material. However, today, all the zinc in a battery comes from a recycled source (although not necessarily from a battery).

What do they recycle?

We did a survey of 203 people and asked them what they recycle. Most people recycle paper and plastic. Far fewer recycle batteries. In the mean time, the number of battery-powered devices continues to increase in the United States and around the world.

Paper Plastic Batteries


Why don't they recycle batteries?

We asked them about their battery recycling behavior. People find is too difficult to find a place to recyle batteries and most don't even know that the laws for battery disposal are in their state. As of January 2016, California and Vermont are the only two states where single-use batteries must be recycled. However, rechargeable batteries must be recycled in all 50 states according to the law! Click here to view the state laws for battery disposal. http://www.call2recycle.org/recycling-law-map/. Note that the map has not been updated for legislation in Vermont.

Sample Survey Comments

I live in a place that doesn't have anywhere to recycle batteries. I've transported them to places up to an hour's drive away to have friends take them away. I've searched and talked to numerous people about it. I still have a drawer of batteries that I can't put in the garbage.

For now, I just keep adding them to the stash on my kitchen counter, hoping that there will be someplace to dispose of them.

In California, it is illegal to throw away batteries. However, it is inconvenient, and not cost-effective. Most locations such as Home Depot or Lowe's or some local recycling centers do NOT recycle alkaline batteries.

There are limited drop off locations, and many of them only accept rechargeable batteries (no alkalines).

If I do get too many alkaline batteries before I am in an office with battery recycling, they will go in the trash. I cannot keep carrying them around.

I don't know any place that is a weekly destination that recycles batteries.

We do recycle large ones that come out of laptops and big games. But the little AA, AAA, 9V, C and D which come out of toys, flashlights and smoke detectors are too difficult to recycle.

Problems in Battery RecyclingWe have identified several problems in the battery recycling industry

Collection

There is certainly a need to improve the collecion process of used batteries. Outside the US, batteries are collected everywhere they are sold. There are collection boxes on the streets, in stores and in schools. In the United States, collection is only in certain stores. These stores are sometimes far away (and not where someone visits weekly). In addition, although all alkalines can be recycled and 100% of an alkaline can be recycled, there are very few places in the US that will collect alkaline batteries.

Education

There is certainly a need for consumer education. 70% of the people we talked to don't even know if there are laws in their states about batteries in the trash. Many consumers are recycling their batteries with their household recyclable bin which is actually not allowed!

Transport

Used battery transportaion is an issue. Lithium batteries can explode. Proper handling of the batteries (taping the leads) costs time and money. This is why many stores charge a fee to recycle batteries.

Costs

Ultimately, the biggest problem we identified is cost. It costs more to recycle an alkaline battery than the materials recovered from them. Lowering these costs begins by looking at the process of battery recycling.

What can you do?Here are some ideas for you.

Recycle Batteries

All battery types can be recycled. They are NOT to be placed in your recycling bin. While one-time use batteries are not dangerous to the environement, they still contain valuable resources. Rechargeable batteries actually contain materials that are harmful to the environment. Finding a place to recycle alkaline batteries is hard. We need to make sure that organizations who are helping to pass legislation hear this and create more places to drop off alkalines.

Education

As your community center, local town offices, schools and libraries if they would be willing to keep a drop off box for spent batteries.
